Mounting Tobii Pro X3–120 Eye Tracker to a screen

 Do NOT connect Tobii Pro X3–120 Eye Tracker to the computer before running the installation software provided on the USB memory stick . Connecting the eye tracker prematurely can lead to the eye tracker not being installed properly!

1

Make sure that the area where the eye tracker is going to be mounted is free from dust and dirt. If you clean it using a cleaning wipe, make sure that the area is completely dry before proceeding with the next step!

2

Choose one of the mounting brackets provided in the Tobii Pro X3–120 Eye Tracker package. Try out the mounting bracket on the screen to see if it works for your screen before attaching it permanently.

1

Measure and locate the middle of the bottom frame of the screen. You will use this as a reference when positioning the mounting bracket

1

Peel off the adhesive tape on the back of the mounting bracket.

2

ATTENTION!
Please be aware that the adhesive tape on the back of the mounting bracket is permanent, meaning that once you attach it to your screen, you won’t be able to remove it!

1

Position the mounting bracket on the bottom frame of your screen. Make sure that it is centered exactly in the middle point of the screen bottom frame as shown in the picture with the narrower side and the indentation facing upward.

1

Apply pressure to the mounting bracket for 30 seconds to make sure it gets attached to the screen.

1

Allow one hour for the adhesive on the mounting bracket to cure and become permanently attached to the screen before attaching the eye tracker unit.

1

Position Tobii Pro X3–120 Eye Tracker and attach it to the mounting bracket as shown in the figure to the right. Make sure to position it in the right way so that the mounting bracket fits exactly in the slot on the back of the eye tracker. The magnet inside will keep the eye tracker in place. The eye tracker USB port should be on the left side of the Tobii Pro X3–120 when attached to the screen.

Specification

The recordings section lists all recordings made and provides the opportunity to manage these recordings. Click on “Recordings” ( screenshot with orange circle around recordings icon) to open the recordings list.
The recordings list contains information about the recordings in the current project.
Details displayed include:
- Recording: The name of the recording
- Participant: The name of the participant
- Duration: The duration of the recording
- Date: The time and date when the recording was performed  

Replaying a recording

1

 Replaying a recording without getting redirected to the Analyze module

If you want to open a recording without getting redirected to the analyze module, right-click on the desired recording and select the option “Open in background”. Pro Lab then will open the recording in the replay tab in the Analyzer module, but you won’t see it until you go to that module by clicking on the “Analyze” option in the top menu bar.

2

 Replaying a recording with getting redirected to the Analyze module

Open and replay a recording in the Recordings list by double-clicking on it or by right-clicking and selecting “Open”. The replay tab will be opened and displayed in the Analyze module.

Deleting a recording

1

ATTENTION!

A deleted recording is permanently removed from the system. Therefore, you must ensure that the recording you wish to delete is no longer needed for anything, because the recording cannot be restored after deletion!

2

Procedure for deleting a recording

- Confirm your choice to delete the item by clicking the “Yes, delete this Recording” option  

Variable Icon

1

Variable

Participant Variables are used to filter data during the process of visualization and comparing the behavior of different participant groups. The created variables and variable values are also included in the data export files which can be used for statistical processing later on. Created participant variables are handled from the participant card in the Record module.

Adding a new participant variable

Procedure

1

If you work Procedure for adding a new participant variable: from the Record tab, click the “Edit variables” button. If you work from the Project Overview window, the Participant Variables panel is already visible, and you can start adding participant variables.

2

If this is the first time you add participant variables, you see a “+ New” button for creating a new participant variable and a generic participant variable. If you already have participant variables defined for this participant, they are displayed, see “6.2.5.3 Edit a participant variable”.

3

Enter the name of the first participant variable in the text field and press Enter.

4

Add a new value for the participant variable by clicking the “+” button. A field appears.

a) Enter the name of the value.
b) Add the wanted values by using the Add Value button.
c) If the participant variable is supposed to accept multiple values, select the “Allow multi selection” check box.
d) You can also add a new value by pressing Enter in a value field.  

5

When you have added the desired values, you can add another participant variable by repeating steps 2–4d.

 Upper Toolbar

Describtion of each icon

1

The Project Overview

Regardless whether you use a screen-based or a wearable eye tracker, the Project Overview section provides information about the elements of your project, such as what recordings are in it and what Events are associated with the recordings. It also provides quick access to some analysis tools.

1

The Design Module

In the Design module, you outline how stimuli are presented to your participants. This includes what the stimuli look like on the screen, in which order they get displayed, as well as how to proceed to the next stimuli. In this module, you also define whether TTL output signals are provided during the stimuli presentation. You can create several different outlines, called Timelines, in the same project. This module is unavailable to projects that don’t use Pro Lab for stimuli presentation, such as wearable eye-tracking projects

1

The Record Module

The Record module is where you make recordings in Pro Lab. It enables you to select which Timeline to use for which recording and lets you control the recording process. This module is unavailable for wearable eye-tracking projects that don’t use the Tobii Pro Glasses Controller for making recordings.

1

The Analyze Module

Analysis is necessary when you want to draw conclusions from the collected data. The Analyze module provides analysis tools that enable you to visualize and export the data you have collected.

The Analyze module is available for all project types. However, the analysis tools might differ somewhat, depending on your use case. 

Heatmaps

Heatmaps help summarize eye-tracking data for reports and papers in an intuitive way

1

1

Click on the Visualization button and select the recording you want to Analyze

1

Select Heatmap in the Visualization Type and Settings Drop-down 

1

In case you need to select a Gaze filter choose “Tobii I-VT (Fixation)” since this preset is optimized for screen-based eye-trackers

2

If needed in the Time of Interest Tool choose a TOI you want to use for your analysis

3

If needed In the Recordings Tool: select a recording that you want analyze via heatmap

1

Click the “Type” drop-down in the visualization interface (screenshot) and choose your calculation basis. There are 4 options:

- Absolute count — calculated by the number of fixations
- Absolute duration — calculated by the duration of fixations
- Relative count — calculated by the number of fixations       relative to the total number of fixations
- Relative duration — calculated by the duration of the fixations relative to the sum of all fixation durations 

2

In order to save the heatmap as an image just right-click on the heatmap and select “save to file..”

Gaze plot

The Gaze Plot Visualization shows the sequence and position of fixations (dots). The size of the dots indicates the fixation duration and the numbers inside the dots represent the order of the fixations. 

1

Click on the Visualization button and select Gaze Plot

2

In case you need to select a Gaze filter choose “Tobii I-VT (Fixation)” since this preset is optimized for screen-based eye-trackers 

1

If needed choose a TOI in the Time of Interest Tool you want to use for your analysis

2

If needed select a recording in the Recordings Tool that you want analyze by means of a gaze plot

3

In order to save the Gaze Plot as an image just right-click on the heatmap and select “save to file...”

Calibration

When starting to record your study, a screen with a calibration task will pop up. The calibration part is relevant for a more accurate gaze capture. Here you have to sit with a suitable distance in front of  

1

When the calibration window pops up, you can choose between to skip it or start it (orange arrow). It is adviced not to skip it

2

The two white balls visualize your eyes (blue arrow). 
When they turn green you are good to go
When they turn red you should try to move a little bit forward or backwards with your torso until it turns green.

3

When the calibration process starts, you should shoud focus your gaze first in on the upper left corner, then the upper right corner, then in the middle, then bottom left corner, and finaly bottom right corner.
